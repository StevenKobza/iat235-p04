ABOUT KALMONT RESCUE TEAM

What We Do

Kalmont Rescue was founded in September 1965. It was initially established as a heavy urban search and rescue unit. Occasionally the local police would ask the team to assist in searching for a lost hiker or skier. Over time it was realized that there was more of a need for a wilderness search and rescue team. Soon, the team started to focus on, and train and equip for, search and rescue operations in the Kalmont mountains.

During its history, Kalmont Rescue has often taken the lead in developing techniques and protocols for Search and Rescue (SAR) operations. Kalmont Rescue was one of the first BC Search and Rescue (SAR) teams to train in human tracking, use search dogs, establish a dive team, and develop special protocols for treating hypothermia victims.

Our Services

Human External Cargo

Kalmont Rescue is a pioneer in Helicopter Human External Cargo System or HEC. HEC is used to insert/extract rescuers into difficult terrain and extract stranded or injured patients. It is quick, efficient and very safe.

Urban Search and Rescue

Kalmont Rescue provides Search and Rescue services to local police agencies in response to searches for elderly walk away’s and lost or missing children. KR has developed a comprehensive pre-plan to deal with this type of demanding urban and rescue emergency.

Civil Emergency Response

Kalmont Rescue has skills to assist in civil emergencies and disasters. The team can provide search and rescue, communications, first aid, and other assistance to the community to alleviate the distress that can be caused by floods, earthquakes, and other natural and man-made events.

Police Evidence Search

Kalmont Rescue is sometimes called upon by the local police to assist in searching for evidence in criminal cases. All KR members are trained in grid searches and securing a scene. The team will work under the direction of a police investigator to search likely areas.

Educational Programs

We create programs for young children, and informative hiking safety lectures tailored to youth and adults. The team has produced a “Hiking Safely” video that is designed to help develop the attitude and safety skills necessary to enjoy hikes with comfort and confidence.

Trail Patrol

Kalmont Rescue provides a nightly sweep of the most popular hiking trails to ensure that the trail is clear of lost or injured hikers. The trails are only swept on nights that the trail is open.
