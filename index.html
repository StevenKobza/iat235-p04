KALMONT RESCUE TEAM

Our Mission

The team consists of approximately 50 volunteers skilled in search and rescue operations in mountain, canyon and urban settings. Kalmont Rescue Team performs approximately 130 search & rescue operations annually, with some operations lasting multiple days. Members participate in different support or rescue categories, depending on individual skills and participation levels. Kalmont Rescue Team is a volunteer community-based search and rescue team. Its members are from all walks of life but share a common interest in providing an important life-saving service to the public, 24 hours a day, every day of the year.

Avoiding Hypothermia

Hypothermia occurs when core body temperature drops below 35 degrees Celsius. Heat is lost in four ways; radiation to a cold environment, conduction through contact with cold surfaces, convection from wind and water currents and evaporation of moisture. Improperly prepared or injured backcountry travellers can become hypothermic even in summer months. Learn about the early warning signs of hypothermia. Use our helpful checklist to find out what you can bring to stay warm on the mountain.

Avalanche Safety Tips

Avalanches might not be the first thing on your mind when you're exploring the mountains, but knowing how to stay safe from avalanches is an important part of travelling in the winter environment. Kalmont rescue gets called to 30 avalanche-related emergencies every year on average. Did you know that most avalanches are caused by hikers and skiiers? Stay safe on the mountain by following our safety tips to recognize dangerous terrain and survive an avalanche.
